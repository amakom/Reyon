Inside our purification process.

Process 4 min read Reyon Editorial

People often assume bottled water is bottled water — that what's inside the label is essentially the same. It isn't. The difference between average bottled water and premium bottled water comes down almost entirely to one thing: the process used to refine it.

At Reyon, our process has three stages. Each one targets a different category of impurity. Together, they produce water that's not just safe, but consistently clean, clear and balanced.

Stage one — Carbon filtration

Raw water arrives carrying a long list of unwanted passengers: chlorine compounds left over from municipal treatment, organic residues, sediment, and the trace odours and tastes that come with all of those.

Activated carbon filters work by adsorption — pulling these molecules onto a vast internal surface area. After this stage, water tastes neutral. There's no chlorine bite, no faint earthiness. Just a clean canvas for the next stage to refine further.

Stage two — Reverse osmosis

This is the heart of our process. Reverse osmosis (RO) pushes water under pressure through a semi-permeable membrane with pores small enough to block dissolved minerals, salts, heavy metals, and microscopic contaminants — while letting only water molecules pass through.

The result is water at near-laboratory purity. No surprise hardness. No mineral imbalances that vary day to day. The water that comes out the other side of our RO stage is ready to be enjoyed — but we're not done yet.

Anyone can filter water. The discipline is in doing it the same way, every time, until consistency becomes the brand itself.

Stage three — UV & ozone disinfection

The final stage exists for one reason: to eliminate any remaining microorganisms. Ultraviolet light disrupts the DNA of bacteria and viruses, rendering them harmless. Ozone gas, dissolved into the water, oxidizes anything else that might have survived the previous two stages.

Both methods are residue-free. Unlike chlorine-based disinfection, they leave nothing behind in the water. What you taste is just water — clean, sterile, and ready to drink.

Quality control behind every batch

The process matters, but so does verification. Every production run is sampled and tested by our quality control team in dedicated laboratory facilities. We check:

  • pH and total dissolved solids
  • Microbial activity
  • Mineral balance
  • Visual clarity

If a batch doesn't meet our internal standard, it doesn't get bottled. Period.

Why we do it this way: the goal isn't to make water that's marginally better than tap. It's to make water that earns the trust of homes, offices and institutions across Nigeria — every bottle, every time. That standard requires a process you can audit. Ours is built that way on purpose.
