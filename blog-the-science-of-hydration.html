The science of staying hydrated.

Wellness 5 min read Reyon Editorial

Hydration is one of those words you hear so often it loses its weight. We're told to drink more water, sip throughout the day, never wait until we're thirsty. But beneath the wellness clichés sits a remarkably simple truth: your body is roughly 60% water, and almost every system that keeps you alive runs on it.

Cells need it to function. Your blood needs it to carry oxygen. Your kidneys need it to filter waste. Your brain needs it to fire signals. When you're under-hydrated, none of those systems stop — they just slow down.

How much water do you actually need?

The familiar advice — eight glasses a day — is a useful baseline, but the real answer depends on you. Body size, climate, activity level, diet, and even how much caffeine you drink all shift the equation.

A more honest framework: aim for 30–35ml per kilogram of body weight per day, then add more if you're sweating, exercising, or in a hot environment. For most adults in Lagos or Warri, that lands somewhere between 2 and 3 litres of total water intake daily — including what's in your food.

Signs you're already behind

By the time you feel thirsty, you're already mildly dehydrated. The earlier signals are quieter — and easy to miss:

  • Light fatigue, especially mid-afternoon
  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Mild headache or "brain fog"
  • Dry lips or mouth
  • Darker urine

None of these are dramatic on their own. Stacked together over a long day, they're the difference between feeling sharp and feeling vaguely off.

Hydration isn't about chasing some perfect number. It's about giving your body what it quietly asks for, before it has to ask loudly.

Why the kind of water matters

Water that's been over-mineralized, contaminated, or carrying chlorine compounds doesn't hydrate the same way clean, balanced water does. The body still uses it, but it has to work harder — and you absorb less of it.

This is why the Reyon process is built around three stages: carbon filtration to clear the path, reverse osmosis to strip what shouldn't be there, and UV/ozone disinfection to ensure the final water is safe down to the microscopic level.

Small habits, big difference

The simplest hydration strategy is the one you'll actually keep. A few habits that consistently work:

  1. Drink a full glass of water within 30 minutes of waking.
  2. Keep a bottle within arm's reach all day. (A 75CL or 1.5L is ideal.)
  3. Drink before meals, not just during them.
  4. Match every cup of coffee or tea with a glass of water.
  5. Track your urine colour — pale yellow is the goal.

The bottom line: hydration is one of the smallest daily inputs with the largest downstream impact on how you feel, focus and perform. The water itself just has to be clean, balanced, and within reach. We make ours that way on purpose.
